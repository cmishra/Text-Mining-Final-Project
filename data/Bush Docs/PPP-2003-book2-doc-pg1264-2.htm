Statement on the Decision by the Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit 
on the Do-Not-Call Registry
October 7, 2003

    Today's decision by the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit 
to allow the FTC to enforce the Do-Not-Call Registry is a victory for 
Americans who want to reduce the nuisance of unwanted telephone 
solicitations. The American people have the right to limit annoying 
telemarketing calls, and I am pleased that both the Federal

[[Page 1265]]

Trade Commission and the Federal Communications Commission will now be 
able to ensure that Americans have that choice while the courts continue 
to consider the issues.
